Frequently Asked Questions help_outline

Who is this for?

Running a full ETL and data warehouse suite is expensive and time-consuming to maintain. If you aren't ready to make this investment, PetalData is your secret sauce. Data scientists and data hobbyists can quickly explore the data in the cloud services you already use without the hassle of figuring out how to access the data.

What is the API?

PetalData is designed to resemble the Python Scikit-Learn datasets module. This gives you a single programatic interface to many data sources.

How does it work?

When you make a call like petaldata.Stripe().load_customers() from our Python package, a request is sent to our PetalData servers. We do the hard, brittle exporting work on our servers. Once the data is exported, we send it down to you in your requested format (ex: Pandas Dataframe) with proper types.

What about security?

We're just as paranoid about leaking data as you are. All network traffic to and from PetalData is via HTTPS. For some large exports, we need to temporarily store data at rest. When we do, this data is encrypted. PetalData does not store credentials for your cloud services.

Why not distribute the entire thing as a Python package and forgoe the server?

Data exporting is a brittle process that requires continual debugging, tweaks, and maintenance. If we didn't have our servers perform the hard work, debugging issues and frequent release cycles would be painful for everyone. We also plans to support other ways to access data outside of Python and it's a lot easier to build out this logic once.

How much does it cost?

Pricing hasn't been finalized, but our intent is to make PetalData affordable for small teams.
